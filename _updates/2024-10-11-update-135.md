Another week, another update. Tari Universe, much like its namesake, continues to expand at a frankly astronomical rate, and we have you to thank for it. The Tari Universe waitlist is sitting over 250,000 people. That said, we'd like to share some stats regarding the known Universe.

* Over 11,800 installs of Tari Universe, and over 6,800 that have actually mined.
* On average, 16% of the 1200 active miners are achieving a win every hour.

Interest in Universe has also been driving parties to the underlying [Tari Suite](https://github.com/tari-project/tari), and as a result our testnet managed to breach [~~over 9000~~](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/It's_Over_9000!) over 800GHs for our SHA3xGPU hashrate at one point, but has settled down to around 650GHs - still incredible number given where we were a couple of days ago.

## New Tari Universe Features
We've been slaving away in the commit mines, and Universe has seen some great new features:
* Like our Mac and Linux versions, Tor is now enabled by default on Tari Universe for Windows users. This has helped resolve many of the network connectivity issues that were impacting user's experience with Tari Universe in the past week. It is important that users understand what Tor is and how it works. You can find out [more about Tor here](https://tlu.tarilabs.com/protocols/intro-to-tor-and-i2p#tor-network).
* We've added CDN mirrors for the dependencies that are called upon when installing and subsequently updating Universe, as well as some additional, automated downloads of unique dependencies in different environments (such the requirement for Visual C++ Redistributable Installer in Windows). This ensures better reliability both installing Universe for the first time and keeping it up-to-date.
* There have been various graphical improvements and user enhancements to the experience of winning blocks, [earning gems](https://github.com/tari-project/universe/pull/682) and [tracking your win history](https://github.com/tari-project/universe/pull/723)





## Minotari - Base Layer
### Ledger Wallet Now Available
While it's been in the releases for a while, we wanted to highlight that we've started releasing a version of our [Ledger](https://www.ledger.com) wallet app for the Nano Plus with our [Minotari releases](https://github.com/tari-project/tari/releases), which can be manually side-loaded on the device. In addition, this week we've added added support for the Stax and Flex devices, although these currently need to built from source and do have some known issues that still need to be addressed. If you would like to know more, you can head over to project and [check the readme.](https://github.com/tari-project/tari/tree/development/applications/minotari_ledger_wallet/wallet) for more details.
